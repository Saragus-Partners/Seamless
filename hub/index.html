Helpful note

You do not need to have every file ready right away. Start with your most recent bank statements and your latest P&L. Those two usually create the clearest first picture.

Bank statements
Where to find these Log in to your bank account, open the business checking account, then look for Statements, Documents, or Account Services. At Chase this is often under “Statements & documents.” At Bank of America look under “Statements & documents” inside the account view. At Wells Fargo it is usually under “Statements & notices.” Download the last 3 to 6 months as PDF.
Upload recent business bank statements. The most useful version is a PDF export directly from your bank.
Last 3 to 6 months preferred
Profit & loss statement
Where to find this In QuickBooks, go to Reports and search for Profit and Loss. In Xero, open Accounting, then Reports, then Profit and Loss. Export the most recent year-to-date view as PDF or Excel.
Upload your latest P&L so the operating picture is easier to understand.
Year-to-date is ideal
Balance sheet
Where to find this In QuickBooks or Xero, search reports for Balance Sheet. Export the most recent month-end version. If your accountant prepared one manually, that version works too.
Upload a current balance sheet if you have one available.
Optional but helpful
Additional supporting files
What can go here Recent tax returns, merchant statements, AR aging reports, equipment quotes, contracts, or anything that explains why you need capital and how the business performs.
